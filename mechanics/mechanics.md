# Core Mechanics

## Exploration
Players navigate the Glitch Museum from a first‑person perspective. Movement includes walking, running and crouching, with a focus on a sense of scale and claustrophobia. Hidden passages and secret compartments reward careful exploration, and some rooms may only appear when viewed from certain angles or after performing specific actions.

## Glitch Lens
An in‑world tool allows the player to perceive underlying data structures. When activated, the lens reveals hidden objects, invisible platforms and debug messages scribbled into the world. It can also temporarily "swap" properties of objects (size, material, gravity) to solve puzzles or access new areas.

## Item Interaction
Almost every prop in the museum can be inspected, picked up or combined. Players can examine glitch artifacts, read old forum printouts or insert floppy disks into terminals to gain lore and hints. Some items trigger scripted events when used in the right context.

## Puzzle Solving
Puzzles are woven into the environment and often require creative use of the glitch lens. Examples include reordering corrupted code snippets to unlock a door, repairing a broken AI by stitching together its dialogue trees, or reversing a corrupted sound file to reveal a password. There is no single solution; multiple approaches encourage experimentation.

## Environmental Corruption
Staying too long in certain rooms or making incorrect choices might increase corruption. As the corruption meter rises, textures distort, audio glitches intensify and pathways shift. Players can find "patches" to temporarily stabilize the space or embrace the chaos to access secret content.

## Progression and Unlocking
Instead of a traditional level structure, the museum functions as a hub. Completing exhibits grants keys, patches or upgrades for the glitch lens. These upgrades allow access to deeper, more corrupted wings. NPCs track your progress and may react differently depending on the order you explore rooms.
