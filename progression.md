# Progression and Structure

Unlike linear games, The Glitch Museum is organized as a web of interconnected exhibits. Players have the freedom to explore wings in different orders, but progression is still guided through gating mechanics and narrative cues.

## Hubs and Wings
The museum is divided into themed wings connected by a central hub. Each wing focuses on a category of artifacts (scrapped games, corrupted files, AI experiments, etc.). Completing puzzles within a wing rewards a "patch key" that unlocks deeper layers of that same wing or grants access to a new wing.

## Glitch Lens Upgrades
Advancement is tied to the glitch lens. Early on, the lens only reveals simple hidden objects. As players solve puzzles, they unlock upgrades (e.g., property swapping, time dilation, parallel reality overlay) that allow them to manipulate the world in more complex ways. Certain rooms are inaccessible until the lens is powerful enough.

## Narrative Checkpoints
Key story beats are anchored at certain exhibits. NPCs provide hints about where to go next, and corrupted terminals display "TODO" notes from the missing developers. Players can piece together the order of events, but the timeline is non‑linear and can be reconstructed in multiple sequences.

## Optional Content
Many exhibits hide optional puzzles and lore that are not required to finish the game but enrich the experience. Discovering hidden rooms or solving meta‑puzzles might unlock alternative endings or cosmetic changes.

## Failing Forward
There is no traditional game over. Mistakes may increase corruption or lock out certain rewards temporarily, but players can always recover by finding stabilizing patches or resetting a room. This design encourages exploration without fear of hard failure.
