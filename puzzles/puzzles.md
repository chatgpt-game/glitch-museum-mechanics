# Puzzle Design

## Categories

### Data Reconstruction
Players encounter broken or incomplete files and must repair them. This could involve dragging and dropping code fragments into the correct order, restoring corrupted images by aligning layers, or reassembling shredded forum posts to glean hidden lore.

### Perspective Shifts
Certain puzzles require the player to view an exhibit from multiple angles or dimensions. Using the glitch lens, players may have to overlay two realities or switch between past and present versions of a room to align symbols or pathways.

### Temporal Loops
Some rooms trap the player in a short time loop. By experimenting with different actions across loops, the player can cause subtle changes that accumulate and eventually break the cycle. Clues may be hidden in audio glitches or NPC dialogue that changes with each iteration.

### Social/ARG Puzzles
Not all puzzles are solved alone. Some exhibits spill over into the real world, requiring players to decode messages from in‑game websites, solve ciphered tweets from fictional developers, or collaborate with other players to input simultaneous commands.

## Sample Puzzle Concepts

1. **Sound Reversal** – In the AI experiments wing, a corrupted audio file emits garbled noise. Players record it, reverse the waveform using an in‑game tool, and recognize a phrase that unlocks a hidden door.

2. **Missing Variables** – A terminal displays a piece of code missing key variables. Players explore the room to find floating variable names and drag them into place. Once compiled, the code spawns a bridge to the next area.

3. **Ghost Dialogue Tree** – In the Internet Archaeology section, an NPC repeats a single line. Players discover archived forum posts that reveal the full conversation tree and must select replies in the correct order to free the NPC.

4. **Physical Redesign** – A room has sliding walls that rearrange when glitched. Players must glitch‑swap properties (weight, friction) of objects to create a stable path while avoiding collapsing geometry.

## Rewards and Feedback
Puzzles reward players with keys, lore entries, glitch lens upgrades or access to secret exhibits. Incorrect attempts often produce amusing or unsettling glitch effects rather than outright failure, encouraging experimentation and discovery.
